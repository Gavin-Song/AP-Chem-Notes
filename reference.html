Solubility Rules

(Hover over ion names to view the formula)

  1. All common salts of elements in Group 1A and the ammonium ion are soluble.
  2. All common acetate, chlorate, perchlorate, nitrate, and nitrite salts are soluble.
  3. All common chloride, bromide, and iodide salts are soluble with the exception of silver, lead and mercury (I)
  4. All common sulfate salts are soluble with the exception of those of silver, lead (II), calcium, barium and strontium.
  5. All carbonates, oxides, phosphates, chromates, and hydroxides are insoluble execpt for those covered by rule 1 (And hydroxides of IIA, strontium and below)
  6. All sulfides are insoluble except for those covered by rule 1 and those of calcium, barium, strontium, and aluminum
* Calcium chromate and calcium and strontium hydroxide are classified as slightly soluble, or even soluble in some textbooks.
** Magnesium sulfide decomposes in water.
*** Most of the oxides of Groups 1 and 2 react with water to form hydroxides.

Strong Acids and Bases

Strong acids:   HCl HBr HI H2SO4 HNO3 HClO4 HClO3*
Strong bases:   LiOH NaOH KOH RbOH CsOH Ca(OH)2 Sr(OH)2 Ba(OH)2

Any acid not listed above is a weak acid
Any base not listed above is a weak base
* Some sources classify HClO3 as a weak acid
